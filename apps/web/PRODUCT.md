# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Neutrino is for developers and platform operators defining, running, reusing, and governing composable AI systems. They use the web app as a control plane for authenticated work such as inspecting persisted platform context, registering and updating manifests, binding services, invoking app actions, and reviewing run, trace, usage, memory, artifact, and integration state.

## Product Purpose

The web app makes picoAI's composable AI service platform operable through a restrained, trustworthy product interface. It should expose the same file-defined resources and runtime records that the platform owns, without creating hidden app-specific structures or bypassing schema, port, auth, and policy boundaries.

Success means a developer can understand the current workspace state, invoke the first vertical path, and inspect what happened with enough clarity to trust the platform model.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, intuitive.

The interface should feel technically serious without becoming cold or bureaucratic. It should help users build confidence through clear state, predictable controls, and direct language rather than ornament.

## Anti-references

This should not look like Salesforce, Oracle, a generic SaaS dashboard, or Supabase.

Avoid dense enterprise chrome, generic analytics-card layouts, decorative glassmorphism, playful AI-chat toy aesthetics, and page-specific visual systems that compete with the shared design system.

## Design Principles

1. Make platform state inspectable before asking users to act.
2. Keep file-defined resources, runtime records, and control-plane actions visibly connected.
3. Prefer earned familiarity over novelty in navigation, forms, tables, and state feedback.
4. Surface scope, identity, permissions, and provenance as first-class product context.
5. Stay restrained: hierarchy, clarity, and operational confidence matter more than decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, visible focus, and form feedback. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone for status, lifecycle state, validation, or destructive actions.
