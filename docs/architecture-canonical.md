## Purpose
Define architecture in one place and enforce implementation conformance for a composable AI service platform.

## Principles
- `All infrastructure integrations must be implemented as adapters behind stable ports.`
- `Application code imports schema and ports, not concrete infrastructure adapters.`
- `app_id is the canonical app identity key across control-plane and runtime surfaces.`
- `Apps are the developer-facing product artifact; apps publish objects, actions, views, and visibility rules.`
- `Services are reusable implementation units behind actions, not first-class product artifacts.`
- `Service identity must be package-style, namespaced, and versioned.`
- `Service definitions are product/platform manifests, not Docker-like container packaging specs.`
- `The minimum internal kernel is Workspace, Org, Project, Actor, Group, Identity, Grant, App, Object, Action, Service, Policy, Schema, Binding, Execution, and Record.`
- `File-defined resources must round-trip with builder UIs instead of creating hidden app-specific resource structures.`
- `Auth, storage, memory, artifacts, catalog, invocation, observability, and policy are platform core concerns.`
- `Postgres is the durable system of record for core state; vector and realtime systems can be swapped.`
- `pgvector is the first vector implementation, not a permanent platform assumption.`
- `ObjectStorage owns artifact bytes; Postgres owns artifact metadata, permissions, and references.`
- `The initial auth service must provide a real login surface and remain swappable toward Ory Kratos and SSO.`
- `Deployment must remain push-to-deploy through CI workflows and IaC-managed environments.`
- `Terraform-managed infrastructure must use a shared remote state backend instead of machine-local state.`
- `The frontend design system is mandatory and built on shared shadcn/ui-based primitives and tokens.`

## Access Model
### Product Vocabulary
- Workspace is the top-level collaboration and billing/security boundary.
- Org is a publisher, customer, vendor, or legal/business entity inside a workspace.
- Project groups app installations and runtime work inside a workspace.
- Actor is anything that can perform an action: a human, service account, app, or system process.
- Group is the Unix-style collection primitive for teams, departments, customers, environments, or cohorts.
- Identity links an actor to a login, SSO account, API key, or service credential.
- Grant records what an actor or group can access.

### Rules
- Do not model member, team_member, role, or permission_set as first-cut schema primitives.
- Represent team and org structure through groups plus grants unless a concrete use case proves a separate table is needed.
- Support SSO through identity records linked to actors, not by special-casing users in product schema.
- Visibility governs app, action, object, view, and service exposure: internal, public, or explicitly allowed workspaces/orgs/groups/actors.

## Service Model
### User-Facing Vocabulary
- Apps publish product surfaces.
- Objects represent app-defined things such as tickets, documents, accounts, or messages.
- Actions mutate or retrieve app-backed data and may be exposed through MCP tools.
- Views render objects with app-defined display metadata where the host supports it.
- Services implement reusable work behind actions.
- Tools provide access.
- Skills guide behavior.
- Policies control who and what is allowed.
- History shows what happened.

### Demoted Product Nouns
- capability
- operation

### Internal Kernel
- `App`: Versioned product package published by an org/workspace that exposes objects, actions, views, and visibility rules.
- `Object`: Small app-defined representation of a thing, with schema and optional display metadata.
- `Action`: App-defined callable operation that can use handlers or reusable services to read or mutate objects.
- `Service`: Versioned reusable implementation unit that can be installed, invoked by actions, donated, and governed.
- `Policy`: Identity, auth, authorization, scope, data boundary, approval, retention, sharing, and audit rules.
- `Schema`: Contracts for inputs, outputs, events, records, stored objects, and service configuration.
- `Binding`: Concrete resolution of a provider, tool, resource, or service dependency in a workspace, project, environment, app, or service scope.
- `Execution`: A single durable unit of work performed by a service.
- `Record`: Append-only typed JSONL fact emitted during execution.

### Service Identity
- Format: `@scope/service-name@version`
- Reserved namespaces:
  - `@pico/*`: First-party platform services and defaults.
- Workspace namespace rule: Workspace and company namespaces such as @acme/* identify company-owned services that can be installed, reused, and governed.
- Shared service rule: Durable shared services should generally use owner or workspace namespaces rather than app namespaces.
- Private service rule: App-private generated services may exist, but must be explicitly marked private or project-scoped.
- Examples:
  - `@pico/agent-builder@1.0.0`
  - `@pico/default-agent-runtime@1.0.0`
  - `@acme/ticket-triage@1.2.0`
  - `@acme/customer-memory@1.0.0`

### Subordinate Concepts
- `Agent`: Service implementation strategy.
- `Harness`: Service/runtime implementation detail or operational scaffold.
- `Skill`: Service or agent implementation asset.
- `Tool`: Binding target or internal dependency.
- `Eval`: Policy-attached check or execution/record validation result.
- `Trace`: Structured records attached to an execution.
- `Artifact`: Input, output, or storage reference attached to an execution.
- `Runtime`: Execution adapter or implementation detail.
- `Workflow`: Service implementation strategy.
- `Capability/action/operation`: Avoid as primary nouns; at most technical service interface details.

### Record And Storage Model
- Append-only JSONL record streams capture canonical chronological facts.
- Postgres owns relational indexes, metadata, execution status, service/package metadata, policy decisions, binding snapshots, and operational source of truth.
- Blob/ObjectStorage owns large inputs, outputs, transcripts, files, traces, and generated artifacts.
- Vector indexes may cover selected records/artifacts only and must inherit policy metadata from source records/artifacts.
- Materialized views may power service history, usage, cost, eval outcome, audit, and product UI summaries.
