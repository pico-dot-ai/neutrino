# Code of Conduct

## Purpose
This document sets clear expectations for how we work together. The goal is a professional, respectful environment where contributors can collaborate effectively across different backgrounds and viewpoints.

## Expected behavior
- Treat people with respect, even in disagreement.
- Focus discussions on the project, the work, and the evidence.
- Assume good intent, ask clarifying questions, and give constructive feedback.
- Acknowledge mistakes and be willing to course-correct.
- Make space for different lived experiences and global perspectives where they intersect with the work.

## Unacceptable behavior
- Harassment, intimidation, or abusive conduct of any kind.
- Personal attacks, name-calling, or targeting an individual or group.
- Deliberately using incorrect names or pronouns after a correction has been made.
- Disparaging, degrading, or excluding people based on identity or background (for example: race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, disability, sexual orientation, or gender identity).
- Persistent off-topic debates that derail project work.
- Sharing private information without explicit permission.

## Enforcement responsibilities
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and will take fair, appropriate action when needed. This may include removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. When possible, maintainers will explain moderation decisions.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, and other collaboration channels) and when representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers at:
- conduct@yourdomain.example

Reports will be reviewed promptly and respectfully, and we will aim for a fair outcome. We will protect the privacy of the reporter to the extent possible.

## Enforcement outcomes
Maintainers may take actions such as a private warning, a request to stop specific behavior, temporary loss of participation privileges, or permanent removal in severe or repeated cases. The response will be proportionate to the situation and aim to keep the project healthy and productive.
